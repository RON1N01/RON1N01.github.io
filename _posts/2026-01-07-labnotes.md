## Lab Notes — Transition to Quality Execution

I’ve been away from posting for close to a month, but activity has not slowed. If anything, it intensified.

Before 2026 arrived, I completed the **TryHackMe Cybersecurity 101** path. That milestone closed the true beginner chapter and positioned me cleanly for **Junior Penetration Tester**, **PT1**, and then **eJPTv2**. Looking further ahead, the plan remains steady and unforced: by the end of 2026, I should be studying OSCP material, without pressure to sit the exam until the timing is right.

The last ~28 days were dense. Not just study, but real-world response, tool building, and operational thinking. This wasn’t passive consumption. It was learning how systems fail, how people respond under pressure, and how small technical decisions compound into real security outcomes.

Because of that, my approach for 2026 is changing.

I’ll be posting less frequently, but with far more intent. No daily noise. No performative consistency. The focus shifts to **qualitative output**:

- Projects with real constraints  
- Thoughtful write-ups  
- CTFs that teach methodology, not tricks  
- Conference attendance and synthesis  
- Tools and documentation that help others, even in small ways  

Certifications remain important markers, but they are not the work itself. Progress is not measured by how much content I consume. It’s measured by:

- Retrieval drills  
- Execution under uncertainty  
- Building and shipping small, useful things  
- Helping colleagues and the wider community operate more securely  

One script that saves time.  
One write-up that clarifies a concept.  
One conversation that raises security awareness.

That’s leverage.

Godspeed.
